About AnthaRaaga

AnthaRaaga is a digital repository designed to document and organize Indian classical rāgas across the Hindustani and Carnatic music systems.

Purpose

The goal of AnthaRaaga is to serve as a structured, repository-first archive of rāgas — focusing on clarity, accuracy, and long-term scalability rather than instruction or performance.

Design Philosophy

The project is intentionally built as a static, data-driven system. Rāgas are treated as structured records, not lessons, allowing the repository to grow across multiple dimensions in the future.

Technology

AnthaRaaga is built using HTML, CSS, and JavaScript, with rāga data stored in a structured JSON format. This approach ensures simplicity, transparency, and ease of future expansion.

Future Scope

The repository is designed to evolve into richer dimensions such as audio references, analytical relationships, system-based comparisons, and API-backed access, while preserving its original structure.

Author

AnthaRaaga is created and maintained by Kaushik Muliya as a long-term digital archive exploring the structure and depth of Indian classical rāgas.
